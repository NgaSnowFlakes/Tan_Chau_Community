About Us

Tan Chau Community Home To Live

In 2017 a decision was made to initiate a Marist Project in the Ta Do Lake area, to work alongside the returnees from Cambodia. Two brothers were assigned to the community, with the hope that the group would be joined by lay people who would live a full community life alongside the brothers. It was to be a 'mixed' community of brothers and lay people. In a very short time young people started to join the group and we quickly became known as a “Marists of Champagnat” community after our founder. The community was based in Tan Chau because the only foreigner in the group had a contract with a local language school and was not permitted to reside near the refugee community. It is hoped that, in the near future, the community residence will move closer to the people they serve. Numbers in the community have varied between 3 at the beginning and at times reached as many as 8 members, some joining for relatively short periods of time (2 months) while others have remained for a year or more.

By: Brother Michael

Ta Do Project Giving - Receiving Love

In recent years, due to difficult living conditions, many Vietnamese who had been living in Cambodia for centuries decided to leave the neighboring country and came back to Viet Nam. Most of these families are fishing for their living, so they all settled at Ta Do village, nearby the lake called Dau Tieng, Tay Ninh province. There has always a demand to cater to young returnees children learning Vietnamese literature and basic mathematics skills, so we organize a program in which we provide these children the opportunity to gain basic literacy and numeracy preparing them for school. Our main objective is to prepare children for school, and in some way it has been met. There is a good number of children were exposed to learning Vietnamese literacy and the ability to read and write themselves.

By: Brother Bao Nguyen
